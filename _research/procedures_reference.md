# Interventional Psychiatry - Procedure Monitoring Reference (research 2026-06-23)

Disclaimer: clinician-facing reference for a prototype. Values from FDA labels/REMS, society guidelines, device manuals, peer-reviewed lit (cited). Many norms are ranges/contested/local-protocol - flagged. Not a device label or local protocol substitute.

## 1. KETAMINE / ESKETAMINE (Spravato)
### IV ketamine (off-label, consensus - Sanacora JAMA Psych 2017)
- Dose: 0.5 mg/kg subanesthetic (range 0.1-0.5 compared; some 0.75-1.0). Optimal contested.
- Infusion: 40 min slow (not bolus). Frequency: acute 2-3/week (~6 over 2-3 wk).
- Monitor: serial BP/HR/SpO2, CADSS, RASS/MOAA-S. BP/HR peak ~30-40 min, resolve ~70-90 min.
### Esketamine (Spravato) REMS (FDA label)
- Restricted REMS: risks of sedation, dissociation, respiratory depression, abuse/misuse. Certified setting only.
- Self-spray under direct observation of HCP. No take-home.
- Monitor >=2 hours after administration each session (incl. pulse oximetry).
- BP: assess pre-dose (baseline); if >140/90 weigh risk/benefit; reassess at ~40 min (Cmax) and as warranted; monitor >=2h; discharge only if BP decreasing + stable.
- Driving: not until next day after restful sleep; arrange transport home.
### Vital thresholds (transient HTN = key AE)
- BP peaks ~40 min, lasts ~4h. ~3-19% had rise >=40 SBP and/or >=25 DBP in first 1.5h.
- Categorical flags: SBP >=180, DBP >=110; rise >=40 SBP / >=25 DBP. Pre-dose hold guide: >140/90.
- Contraindicated: aneurysmal vascular disease, AVM, prior ICH.
### Sedation scales
- RASS (+4..-5): +4 combative, +3 very agitated, +2 agitated, +1 restless, 0 alert/calm, -1 drowsy, -2 light sedation, -3 moderate, -4 deep, -5 unarousable.
- MOAA/S (0-5, used in Spravato trials): 5 responds readily to name, 4 lethargic, 3 responds to loud/repeated name, 2 responds to mild prod/shake, 1 responds to painful trapezius squeeze, 0 no response. Any decrease from pre-dose = sedation.
- Ramsay (1-6): 1 anxious/agitated, 2 cooperative/tranquil, 3 responds to commands, 4 asleep brisk glabellar response, 5 sluggish, 6 no response.
### Other AEs: dizziness ~29%, nausea, sedation ~23%, vertigo ~28%, anxiety, BP increased ~10%, dysgeusia ~19%, headache. Craving/abuse (Schedule III).
Sources: FDA Spravato PI (accessdata 211243s016); spravatorems.com; Sanacora JAMA Psych 2017; Sessler RASS 2002; Bremner CADSS 1998.

## 2. ECT
### Seizure duration
- Motor (cuff/Hamilton): historical adequate >=15s. Can be absent (~6%) with adequate EEG.
- EEG: adequate convention >=20-30s (often >=25s). Primary monitor.
- Prolonged: >120s consider terminating; >180s = status, terminate. Higher risk: theophylline, lithium, bupropion.
- Cuff (Hamilton): BP cuff inflated above systolic BEFORE succinylcholine to time convulsion / confirm generalization (unilateral).
- NUANCE: duration is necessary-but-not-sufficient, weak quality marker. Classic 15/25s cutoffs have little prognostic benefit. Weigh quality > duration.
### Stimulus dosing
- Dose = charge in millicoulombs (mC). Charge = current x pulse width x freq x train duration.
- Current ~800-900 mA. Pulse width: brief 0.5-2 ms; ultrabrief 0.25-0.3 ms. Freq ~10-120 Hz. Train ~0.2-8s.
- Device max: Thymatron System IV 504 mC (~99J), 1008 double-dose; MECTA Spectrum ~576 mC (up to ~1152). Confirm device.
- Seizure threshold (ST) varies ~40-fold, rises with age/male/bilateral. Empirical titration session 1, then fixed multiple above ST.
- Placement: RUL ~5-6x ST (ultrabrief 6-8x) - least cognitive; Bitemporal ~1.5x ST - most robust + most cognitive; Bifrontal ~1.5x ST - intermediate.
### Anesthetic + relaxant (mg/kg IV)
- Methohexital 0.5-1.0 (reference, near threshold-neutral); Propofol 0.75-1.0 (potently anticonvulsant, shortens seizure); Etomidate 0.15-0.3 (longest seizures, myoclonus/PONV); Ketamine 0.5-2 (lengthens, HTN/tachy). Succinylcholine 0.5-1.0 (NMB; avoid if hyperkalemia/burns/MH -> rocuronium+sugammadex).
- Threshold (lower=longer): Ketamine > Etomidate > Methohexital > Sevoflurane > Thiopental > Propofol.
### Ictal quality indices (predict response > duration; none a validated standalone cutoff)
- Postictal suppression index (PSI) - strongest single marker (higher=better). Peak/avg/midictal amplitude. Seizure energy index. Ictal/interhemispheric coherence (max sustained). Recruitment phase/time-to-peak. Sympathetic/peak HR response.
### Cognitive/recovery
- Reorientation time (ROT): ~5-45 min; >30 min = red flag (worse retrograde memory, Sobin). Track each session.
- Postictal confusion/agitation; anterograde amnesia (resolves ~2mo); retrograde autobiographical amnesia (main durable concern); subjective memory complaints (weakly correlated, corroborate objectively).
- Cognitive burden: RUL ultrabrief < RUL brief < bifrontal ~ bitemporal.
Sources: Indian ECT CPG PMC10096214; motor-vs-EEG PMC2953649; Sackeim NEJM 1993; ictal EEG S1935861X20302485; anesthesia StatPearls NBK576431; Sobin PubMed 7793470; Thymatron/MECTA manuals.

## 3. TMS (rTMS L-DLPFC for depression)
### Resting Motor Threshold (RMT)
- Lowest %MSO evoking MEP >=50 uV peak-to-peak in >=5 of 10 trials in relaxed APB/FDI. Visual-twitch surrogate overestimates -> prefer EMG; document method.
- Treatment intensity = % of RMT: standard 10 Hz = 120% RMT (range ~80-120%); SAINT = 90% RMT depth-adjusted.
### Standard 10 Hz L-DLPFC (NeuroStar)
- 10 Hz, 120% RMT, 4s train (40 pulses), 26s ITI, 75 trains, ~3000 pulses/session, ~37.5 min. Course daily 5x/wk, 4-6 wk = 20-30 sessions + taper.
- BrainsWay Deep TMS (H1): 18 Hz, 120% RMT, 2s train, 20s ITI, 55 trains, 1980 pulses.
### iTBS (theta burst, FDA-cleared 2018)
- 3 pulses at 50 Hz, bursts at 5 Hz; 2s on / 8s off; 600 pulses; ~3 min; 120% RMT. THREE-D (Blumberger 2018 Lancet): non-inferior to 10 Hz.
- SAINT/SNT (accelerated, fMRI-targeted, FDA-cleared 2022): 10 sessions/day x 5 days (50 total); 1800 pulses/session (90,000 total); 90% RMT depth-adjusted.
### Target localization: 5cm rule (>20mm off in ~54%); Beam F3 (better, contested superiority); MRI neuronavigation (~4mm, required for fMRI targets).
### Safety/IFCN
- AEs: scalp pain/discomfort (most common, habituates), headache, transient hearing (earplugs), vasovagal syncope, rare seizure, uncommon hypomania.
- Seizure risk: Rossi 2009 <1% healthy; large survey ~8/100,000 sessions, <2/100,000 within guidelines.
- IFCN max safe single-train (s): 10Hz @120% RMT = 4.2s; ITI >=5s for HF rTMS.
- Contra/precaution: ferromagnetic/electronic hardware near coil (cochlear implant, IPG/DBS/VNS, aneurysm clips), pacemaker/ICD caution, epilepsy/head-trauma, threshold-lowering meds, pregnancy precaution. >=2 trained staff + emergency capability; hearing protection; log intensity/pulses; re-measure RMT periodically.
Sources: Rossi 2009 & 2021; Perera 2016 Brain Stimul; Blumberger THREE-D 2018; Cole SAINT 2020; NeuroStar PMC3159591; safety PMC9879417; Taylor 2021.

## 4. DBS (psychiatric)
### Regulatory: depression (TRD) DBS is INVESTIGATIONAL (not FDA-approved). Only psychiatric authorization = HDE H050003 (Medtronic Reclaim) for treatment-resistant OCD, bilateral ALIC/VC-VS, approved 2009-02-19. Largest depression RCT (RECLAIM, Dougherty 2015) was NEGATIVE.
### Awake intra-op testing
- Per-contact macrostimulation: step through each contact, log acute benefit vs side effects -> therapeutic window (benefit threshold vs side-effect threshold). Monopolar review. Behavioral macrostim (acute mood/mirth per contact) often decisive.
### Per-contact parameters
- Amplitude: V (voltage-controlled) or mA (constant-current). Movement ref ~1-4. Psych trials: SCC ~2-8V (~4), VC/VS ~6-8V (~6.7), NAcc ~2-10V, slMFB ~1-3.5mA (~3.0).
- Pulse width (us): movement ~60 (60-90); SCC 60-210 (~90); VC/VS & NAcc 90-210; ITP ~450.
- Frequency (Hz): movement ~130 (100-185); SCC ~110-140 (mostly 130); VC/VS ~100-130 (~127); NAcc ~130; slMFB ~125-130.
- Config: monopolar first, switch to bipolar to widen window.
- Anchor trials: SCC/Cg25 (Holtzheimer 2012) 130 Hz/91 us/4 mA monopolar. VC/VS OCD intra-op 0/2/4/6/8 V, 210 us, 135 Hz. slMFB (Schlaepfer/Coenen) 1-3 mA/130 Hz/60 us.
### Therapeutic-window effects to chart per contact
- Mood positive: calmness, brightening, mirth/smile/laughter/euphoria (VC/VS hallmark; distinguish transient mirth from sustained hypomania).
- Mood negative: acute sadness, tearfulness/crying, anxiety (SCC/Cg25, VC/VS) - reversible w/ parameter change.
- Sustained/behavioral: hypomania (~39% VC/VS), agitation (~30%), impulsivity (~19%).
- Sensory: paresthesia. Motor: muscle contraction (capsular spread), dysarthria. Autonomic: sweating, flushing, nausea, HR/BP change. Gaze/oculomotor: gaze deviation, diplopia, blurred vision, oscillopsia (slMFB hallmark).
### Targets: SCC/Cg25/BA25 (depression, investig.); VC/VS (OCD HDE, depression investig.); ALIC (OCD HDE); NAcc; ITP (~450us); slMFB; anteromedial STN (OCD).
Sources: Mayberg Neuron 2005; Holtzheimer 2012; Dougherty RECLAIM 2015; FDA HDE H050003; Frizon dosing PMC6050377; Haq/Greenberg/Okun PMC2907450; Coenen slMFB PMC6785007.
